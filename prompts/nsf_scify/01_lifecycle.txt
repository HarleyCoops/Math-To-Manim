Paper grounding from arXiv:2503.08600: NSF-SciFy is built from the NSF Awards XML database, covering awards from 1970 through September 2024. After parsing, the authors obtain 412,155 parseable awards. The full dataset contains about 2.8 million scientific claims from roughly 400,000 NSF award abstracts across science and mathematics. Each record may include award ID, title/year, directorate/division, technical abstract, non-technical abstract, extracted scientific claims, extracted investigation proposals, and linked publications. Non-technical abstracts are present in about 81% of awards. Materials science subset: 16,042 awards, 114K claims, 145K investigation proposals, and 2,953 awards with linked publications. NSF-SciFy-20K: 20,001 awards across MPS, GEO, ENG, CSE, BIO with 135K claims and 139K investigation proposals.

Conceptual payload: a grant abstract is an early fossil record of scientific intent. NSF-SciFy turns prose into a typed record: what the proposal claims is true, what it plans to investigate, and what later publications may connect back to it. The viewer should see scientific truth as a time-indexed graph: proposal → claim/proposed work → publications → verification.

Palette: dark archive #060A14, formula white #F8FAFC, NSF blue #2563EB, claim gold #FACC15, investigation violet #A78BFA, publication green #34D399, verification red #FB7185, quiet gray #64748B.
