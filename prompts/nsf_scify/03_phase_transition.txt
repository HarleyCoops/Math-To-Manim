Paper grounding from arXiv:2503.08600: NSF-SciFy is used to fine-tune Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 and Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct on three tasks: technical-to-non-technical abstract generation, abstract-to-scientific-claims extraction, and abstract-to-investigation-proposals extraction. The materials dataset is deduplicated and filtered to 11,141 examples, split into 8,641 train, 500 validation, and 2,000 test. Fine-tuning uses LoRA with rank \(128\), \(\alpha=64\), learning rate \(10^{-5}\), 3 epochs, 100 warmup steps, batch size 2 with 4 gradient accumulation steps, updating attention projections and MLP gate/up/down layers. Results: Task 1 improves modestly because base models are already strong: Mistral BERTScore-F1 \(0.8561\), +0.36%. Task 2 claim extraction improves dramatically: Mistral precision \(0.7450\), recall \(0.7098\), F1 \(0.7097\), F1 +101.8%. Task 3 investigation proposal extraction improves sharply: Mistral precision \(0.7351\), recall \(0.7539\), F1 \(0.7261\), F1 +90.97%.

Conceptual payload: show fine-tuning as a phase transition in semantic geometry. Before supervision, claims, proposals, and background prose are a noisy gas. After NSF-SciFy supervision, the latent space condenses into two usable decision basins. The aha moment is crossing a visible threshold where extraction becomes an instrument rather than a guess.
