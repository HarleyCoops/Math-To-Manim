Paper grounding from arXiv:2503.08600: for claim extraction, the paper evaluates a generated set \(S\) against a gold set \(G\) using a pairwise boolean judge function \(\Phi_{claim}\), implemented with GPT-4o-mini, to determine whether generated claim \(c\) is supported by gold claim \(g\). Precision averages over generated claims, taking the best gold match. Recall averages over gold claims, taking the best generated match. The paper notes this is needed because the task compares sets of claims, not one claim against one document, and because it must penalize spurious generated claims and missing gold claims. Repeats and near-repeats are removed by thresholding cosine similarity over TF-IDF representations. The same structure is used for investigation proposals with \(\Phi_{IP}\). The authors manually validated a subset and found near-perfect correlation for the pairwise comparison. Across directorates, extracted claims show consistently high precision but lower recall; investigation proposals are more balanced.

Conceptual payload: make the metric geometry intuitive. Generated claims and gold claims are two point clouds. The judge draws support edges. Precision asks “does every generated point land near some gold point?” Recall asks “does every gold point get covered by some generated point?” The aha moment is that the formulas are two opposite sweeps through the same bipartite graph.
